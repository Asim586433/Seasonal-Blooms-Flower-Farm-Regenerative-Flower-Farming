Our Farming Values

We believe flowers should be grown with care for the earth and community.

Regenerative Practices

We use no-till methods, cover crops, and compost to build soil health and sequester carbon.

Water Stewardship

Drip irrigation and rainwater capture minimize our water usage and protect local watersheds.

Chemical-Free

We never use synthetic pesticides or herbicides, creating a safe habitat for pollinators and people.

Seasonal Focus

We grow what naturally thrives in our climate and season, reducing energy-intensive practices.

Our Farming Story

Growing flowers regeneratively on 5 acres in the Pacific Northwest since 2018.

From Lawn to Flower Farm

Seasonal Blooms began as a passion project on a suburban lawn and has grown into a 5-acre regenerative flower farm. Founded by Maya and Sam in 2018, we transformed depleted pastureland into a thriving ecosystem that produces beautiful blooms while restoring the soil.

Our approach is simple: work with nature, not against it. We prioritize soil health, biodiversity, and seasonal rhythms over high yields and convenience. This means our offerings change throughout the year, reflecting what's naturally thriving in our fields.

We're not just growing flowers; we're growing community and connection to the land. We host workshops, welcome volunteers, and share our knowledge with other aspiring flower farmers.

Our Growing Practices

No-Till Methods

We minimize soil disturbance to preserve soil structure and microbiology.

Pollinator Habitat

15% of our land is dedicated to native plants that support bees and butterflies.

Closed-Loop Composting

All plant waste is composted and returned to our fields as nutrient-rich humus.

Hand-Harvested

Every stem is carefully cut by hand at the perfect stage of bloom.
